Mysterious Creatures in Belarusian and British Folklore
Protectors and Helpers

Kalyakh-vare is a well-known witch of Scottish folklore, the spirit of winter and cold, the patroness of wild animals and fish. It is a skinny witch with a blue face. On the eve of each Halloween Kalyakh-vare is born again and then walks on the ground causing snow. On the eve of the first of May it leaves and turns into a grey stone. Kalyakh-vare protects lots of different animals. Most of all it likes deer. They are its cattle. It herds and milks them, often saves them from hunters.

Dobie is a kind of Brownie. It helps people with household chores. But be ready that eggs will be broken, milk will be poured and so on.

In English folklore Hobgoblin is a goodnatured house fairy similar to brownie. It is about two feet high and they have dark skin. They walk either naked or in dark clothes. They seldom leave the house, preferring to get warm by the fire. However, they are very sensitive and offensive. They can make milk sour, tear the clothes and make a purely swept floor dirty.

Bug is a fairy spirit in English mythology. They frighten children. The appearance of bugs can be various. Usually these beings have an appearance of shaggy mon-sters with matted ragged wool. Some people compare them with brown bears. Many English children believe that bugs can get into rooms through open chimneys. However these creatures aren't aggressive and almost harmless. They have neither sharp teeth, nor long claws. Some bugs have quite frightening own names – Jannie Green Teeth, Skin - And - Bone, Tom - Look - in a - Chink. Each of them looks in its own way and carries out certain duties: for example, bug Lazy Laurence protects gardens from boys’ attacks.

Killmulis lives in the northern areas of England. He lives in mills. This fairy has no mouth for some reason, but there is a very big nose that can smell food at a very long distance. Killmulis helps a miller with everything, cares for his wellbeing.

The English fairy with quite unusual name Klabauterman lives in bow figures of sailing ships. According to legends Klabauterman lives in trees. When the tree is cut and a bow figure of the vessel is made of it these creatures get inside and go sailing in the sea. Klabauterman is usually no more than a meter high. He wears a red jacket and a round red hat. Sometimes Klabauterman wears yellow or white trousers and big sized high boots. Klabauterman often helps ship carpenters.
