About

The actuality of this project is connected with a rising interest of adults and children towards books, cartoons, movies and Internet games where the main charac-ters are different mysterious creatures: elves, fairies, goblins, orcs and others. As a matter of fact, most of them are of British origin. So we decided to find more infor-mation about them and to compare them with Belarusian creatures. During our research we have learned the information about more than 100 mythical creatures of Belarusian folklore and about 500 creatures of British folklore. British folklore is wide as it includes mythological representatives of England, Scot-land, Wales and Ireland. It explains the superiority in the number of mysterious crea-tures. Besides folklore is a part of culture and history of any country. The knowledge of history and cultural traditions is an integral part of studying foreign languages. Myths, legends, poems, songs and ballads reflect the development and formation of the country and the mentality of its people. The material of our research work is both educational and entertaining. It can be used for English extra classes to increase students’ interest and their level of knowledge.

Results of this work
The history and the culture of each country have their unique myths and leg-ends. Britain and Belarus are not an exception. Their myths and legends are rich in different spirits and mysterious creatures. People have always been interested in them. Though not so many of them believe in these creatures nowadays, the majority of people still prefer watching films, cartoons, playing computer games and reading books where the main characters are different mysterious beings. So we decided to summarize the information about them and create the Internet site “Mysterious Crea-tures”. This site will be interesting for children and adults as well. The language of our site is English. So it gives the opportunity to deepen your knowledge of English.
